AP Computer Science A · Data Unit

Pivot Tables
a four-day unit

A hands-on introduction to grouping, aggregation, and data storytelling — built around simulated Michigan traffic crash data from 2022 to 2024, capped by a week-long mini-project on a dataset of your choice.

500  records 15  columns 10  counties 4  days

The Unit

Pick a day to begin

Each day is a self-contained interactive page. Work through the lessons, answer the questions, then download a PDF of your work to upload to Google Classroom.

Day 1  ·  Introduction

From scratch — rows, values, and the first pivot table

Understand the dataset, learn to build a pivot table in Google Sheets, and answer six questions using row groupings and aggregations.

Open Day 1 Day 2  ·  Filters & Cross-tabs

Sharper questions — narrowing the data, two ways

Add filters to slice the dataset, build cross-tabs that group on two dimensions at once, and write your own finding from the data.

Open Day 2 Day 3  ·  Visualizing

Charts from pivots — picking the right shape

Turn pivot tables into charts. Learn when a bar chart, stacked bar, or line chart tells the truth — and when it lies.

Open Day 3 Day 4  ·  Mini-Project

Your question, your data — a week-long capstone

Pick your own dataset, frame a question, analyze with pivots, and present what you found. A full week from choosing to sharing.

Open Day 4
Three days on one dataset, one week on yours.  Days 1–3 use the same 500 Michigan traffic crash records — the patterns you discover on Day 1 are the foundation for the filters, cross-tabs, and charts you'll build on Days 2 and 3. Day 4 turns the lens on a dataset you choose.
